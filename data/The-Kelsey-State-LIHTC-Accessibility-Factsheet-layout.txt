Disability-Forward LIHTC Reform

The population of older adults and people with disabilities is rapidly growing. We cannot wait
for all 50 states to implement individual standards. The federal government can ensure that no
matter what state disabled people live in they have accessible housing by requiring that LIHTC
follow the Uniform Federal Accessibility Standards (UFAS).

There can also be impactful incentives for projects that construct accessible or adaptable units
beyond what is required by UFAS, utilizing resources like The Housing Design Standards for
Accessibility and Inclusion. These requirements and incentives will not only drastically increase
the supply of affordable, accessible, and inclusive housing, but they will also create uniformity
and clear guidelines for the affordable housing industry regarding access.

For more information on federal and state data and disability-forward LIHTC advocacy, contact
