This report catalogs empirical claims made in the memo that rely on external data sources and should be verified for accuracy before publication. Claims are organized by subject area with the cited source and the specific assertion to check.

## II. Housing Stock and Accessibility Data

### 12. 70% of newly constructed multifamily properties violate FHA design-and-construction requirements
- **Claim (Part IV.A, fn118/179):** "The Equal Rights Center found that 70% of newly constructed multifamily properties violate even these minimal requirements."

### 14. 845,000 LIHTC units will lose affordability protections by 2035
- **Claim (Part IV.C, fn174):** "845,000 LIHTC units will lose affordability protections by 2035."

### 15. <35,000 Section 811 units exist for 4.1 million working-age disabled SSI recipients

## VII. Claims Involving Author's Own Analysis or Inference

These are not pure citation-checks but claims where the author draws inferences from data that should be scrutinized for logical validity:

### 44. "74-82% of disability complaints involved paradigmatic transactional violations"
- **Derivation:** From the ~45% reasonable-accommodation share of *all* complaints applied to disability's ~55-66% share.
- **Check the math:** If 45% of all complaints are RA and disability is 54.59-65.97% of all complaints, then RA as a share of disability complaints = 45/54.59 to 45/65.97 = 68.2% to 82.4%. The memo says "seventy-four to eighty-two percent" -- verify the lower bound derivation.

### 45. Convergence thesis: disability enforcement produces racially progressive distributional outcomes
- **Claim (Part II.E):** Characterized as "the strongest available inference from existing data, advanced as a falsifiable hypothesis."
- **Check:** The memo correctly qualifies this as inference from two-way tabulations, but verify that the underlying data points (claims 18, 22, 23 above) are accurate, since the inference depends on them.

### 46. CDBG = 0.1 to 2% of municipal budgets
- **Claim (Part III.A, fn91):** Compared to *South Dakota v. Dole*'s "five percent" coercion threshold.
- **Verify:** Confirm the 0.1-2% range for CDBG's share of municipal budgets. The footnote references *Dole* but the percentage range itself needs a source.
