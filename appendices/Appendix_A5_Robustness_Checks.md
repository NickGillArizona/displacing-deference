## Appendix A-5: Robustness and Sensitivity Checks

### A-5.1 Overview

This appendix reports five formal sensitivity analyses testing the robustness of the composition-effect finding reported in Part II.D. The central claim is that 76% of the P1-to-P3 aggregate win-rate decline is attributable to the composition shift (the pro se filing surge) rather than to within-group rate changes. Each check addresses a distinct threat to that inference. All results are generated by `scripts/robustness_checks.py` using the FHA Unified Database; full console output is preserved in `results/robustness_checks_output.txt`.

### A-5.2 Check 1: Reclassification Sensitivity

**Threat addressed:** Pipeline misclassification of procedural outcomes could inflate represented win rates if procedural dispositions are disproportionately coded as non-losses.

**Method:** All 302 non-decided outcomes (procedural and settlement dispositions) are reclassified as defendant wins — the maximally pessimistic assumption.

**Results:**

| Metric | P1 Base | P1 Recl. | P2 Base | P2 Recl. | P3 Base | P3 Recl. |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| N decided | 456 | 615 | 116 | 159 | 317 | 417 |
| Aggregate strict win % | 18.0 | 13.3 | 7.8 | 5.7 | 10.7 | 8.2 |
| Pro se strict win % | 7.3 | 5.5 | 1.4 | 1.1 | 4.4 | 3.4 |
| Represented strict win % | 34.3 | 24.5 | 19.0 | 11.6 | 34.3 | 23.7 |

**Finding:** The P1-to-P3 delta in represented win rate is -0.8 percentage points (24.5% vs. 23.7%) under worst-case reclassification. The composition-effect finding **holds**: the represented win rate is essentially stable across periods even when every ambiguous outcome is counted against plaintiffs.

### A-5.3 Check 2: Period-Boundary Sensitivity

**Threat addressed:** The three-period structure depends on the placement of the P2/P3 boundary (February 2025). If the finding is an artifact of boundary selection, it should not survive shifts.

**Method:** The P2/P3 boundary is shifted ±2 months; the P1/P2 boundary is shifted ±2 months. Key metrics are recomputed for each specification.

**P2/P3 boundary results:**

| Boundary | P2 N | P3 N | P3 Pro Se % | Pro Se Win % | Rep. Win % | Agg. Win % |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dec. 2024 (early) | 123 | 453 | 76.2 | 4.1 | 32.4 | 10.2 |
| Feb. 2025 (original) | 159 | 417 | 76.7 | 4.4 | 34.3 | 10.7 |
| Apr. 2025 (late) | 224 | 352 | 79.0 | 4.2 | 32.0 | 9.5 |

**P1/P2 boundary results:**

| Boundary | P1 N | P2 N | P1 Pro Se % | P1 Rep. Win % |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Apr. 2024 (early) | 565 | 209 | 58.6 | 34.7 |
| June 2024 (original) | 615 | 159 | 58.9 | 34.3 |
| Aug. 2024 (late) | 676 | 98 | 57.1 | 32.7 |

**Finding:** The composition-effect finding is **stable** across all boundary specifications. P3 pro se share ranges from 76.2% to 79.0% (always elevated), and P3 represented win rate ranges from 32.0% to 34.3% (always comparable to P1). Moving the P2/P3 boundary to April 2025 actually strengthens the pro se share finding (79.0%).

### A-5.4 Check 3: Category Exclusion

**Threat addressed:** Rare defendant types or plaintiff types with small cell sizes could exert disproportionate influence on aggregated rates.

**Method:** Defendant types and plaintiff types with fewer than 60 cases (5% of the 1,191-case dated population) are excluded. This removes 197 cases (16.5%).

**Results:**

| Period | N | Pro Se % | Pro Se Win % | Rep. Win % | Agg. Win % |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | 501 | 64.7 | 7.5 | 35.6 | 17.2 |
| P2 | 130 | 62.3 | 1.5 | 15.6 | 6.0 |
| P3 | 363 | 80.2 | 4.8 | 30.6 | 9.4 |

**Finding:** The composition-effect finding **holds** after excluding uncommon categories. The pro se share in P3 is actually higher (80.2% vs. 76.7% baseline), and the represented win rate remains comparable to P1 (30.6% vs. 35.6%, a modest 5 pp gap well within sampling variability given n=67 for P3 represented decided cases).

### A-5.5 Check 4: Bootstrap Confidence Intervals

**Threat addressed:** Point estimates from small subsamples (particularly P3 represented, n=67 decided) may be imprecise.

**Method:** 10,000 bootstrap resamples with replacement; 95% confidence intervals computed from the 2.5th and 97.5th percentiles. Random seed: 42.

**Results:**

| Group | Point Est. | 95% CI | N Decided |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 represented | 34.3% | [27.6, 41.4] | 181 |
| P3 represented | 34.3% | [23.9, 46.3] | 67 |
| P1 pro se | 7.3% | [4.4, 10.5] | 275 |
| P3 pro se | 4.4% | [2.0, 7.2] | 250 |
| P1 aggregate | 18.0% | [14.5, 21.5] | 456 |
| P3 aggregate | 10.7% | [7.6, 14.2] | 317 |

**Pro se share CIs:**

| Period | Point Est. | 95% CI | N |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | 58.9% | [55.0, 62.8] | 615 |
| P3 | 76.7% | [72.7, 80.8] | 417 |

**Findings:**
- P1 and P3 represented win rate CIs **overlap completely** ([27.6, 41.4] vs. [23.9, 46.3]), confirming the represented win rate is statistically indistinguishable across periods.
- P1 and P3 pro se share CIs **do not overlap** ([55.0, 62.8] vs. [72.7, 80.8]), confirming the composition shift is statistically real.

### A-5.6 Check 5: Statistical Significance and Oaxaca-Blinder Decomposition

**Threat addressed:** The composition-effect claim requires formal decomposition, not just descriptive observation.

**Chi-squared tests:**

| Test | Chi-squared | p-value | Significant? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pro se share P1 vs. P3 | 35.43 | < 0.0001 | Yes (p < 10⁻⁸) |
| Represented win rate P1 vs. P3 | 0.0001 | 0.991 | No (no change) |
| Aggregate win rate P1 vs. P3 | 7.72 | 0.005 | Yes |

**Oaxaca-Blinder decomposition:**

The Oaxaca-Blinder decomposition partitions an aggregate outcome gap into a "composition effect" (attributable to differences in the distribution of observable characteristics across periods) and a "rate effect" (attributable to differences in the within-group returns to those characteristics). See Ronald Oaxaca, *Male-Female Wage Differentials in Urban Labor Markets*, 14 Int'l Econ. Rev. 693 (1973); Alan S. Blinder, *Wage Discrimination: Reduced Form and Structural Estimates*, 8 J. Hum. Resources 436 (1973).

| Component | Value | Share of Decline |
|---|---|---|
| P1 weighted aggregate | 18.4% | — |
| P3 weighted aggregate | 11.4% | — |
| Counterfactual (P3 rates, P1 composition) | 16.7% | — |
| **Total decline** | **-7.1 pp** | **100%** |
| **Composition effect** | **-5.3 pp** | **76%** |
| **Rate effect** | **-1.7 pp** | **24%** |

**Finding:** 76% of the aggregate win-rate decline is attributable to the composition shift; only 24% to within-group rate changes. The represented plaintiff win rate shows literally no statistically significant change between P1 and P3 (p = 0.991). The aggregate decline is a demographic phenomenon driven by the pro se filing surge, not a judicial one.

### A-5.7 Summary

All five robustness checks confirm the composition-effect finding:

1. **Reclassification**: Finding holds when all procedural outcomes are counted as defendant wins (-0.8 pp represented delta).
2. **Period boundaries**: Finding is stable across ±2-month boundary shifts in both P1/P2 and P2/P3 boundaries.
3. **Category exclusion**: Removing rare categories actually strengthens the pro se share finding (80.2% in P3).
4. **Bootstrap CIs**: Represented win rate CIs overlap completely across periods; pro se share CIs do not overlap.
5. **Oaxaca-Blinder**: 76% composition effect, 24% rate effect; represented win rate change p = 0.991.

The composition-effect claim is the Note's most robust empirical finding. It does not depend on any single methodological choice, boundary placement, or classification assumption.
