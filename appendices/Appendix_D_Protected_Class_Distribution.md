# Appendix D: Protected-Class Distribution

The FHA Pilot Database (n=331 cases, 2012-2026) provides the cross-class comparison underlying the Note's observation that disability constitutes the plurality of federal FHA litigation. Protected-class distribution across the pilot corpus:

| Protected Class | Cases | Share |
|----------------|:-----:|:-----:|
| Disability | 126 | 38.1% |
| Race | 98 | 29.6% |
| Familial Status | 42 | 12.7% |
| National Origin | 33 | 10.0% |
| Religion | 18 | 5.4% |
| Sex | 14 | 4.2% |

The RA Database (n=1,857 cases, 2021-2026) confirms the pattern in the more recent period. For the full RA Database protected-class breakdown, see Appendix A, Section A.4.

The disability share (38.1%) in the pilot database is notably lower than the 54.6% administrative complaint share reported in the NFHA 2025 Trends Report. This gap likely reflects two factors: (1) the pilot search terms ("fair housing act" AND "race" AND "zoning") were designed to favor race-and-zoning cases, biasing the sample against disability; and (2) disability complaints resolve at higher rates through conciliation and voluntary compliance, reducing the share that reaches federal court.

For the Unified Dataset's per-claim breakdown across all protected classes, see the `results/unified_stats.json` file in the Data Repository.
