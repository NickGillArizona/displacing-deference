## Appendix B: Three-Period Post-*Loper Bright* Analysis

### B.1 Three-Period Comparison — FHA Unified Database, Disability Cases (n=1,720)

**Periods:** P1: January 1, 2022 -- June 28, 2024 (pre-*Loper Bright*); P2: June 28, 2024 -- February 5, 2025 (transition); P3: February 5, 2025 -- present (post-enforcement-withdrawal).

| Period | N decided | PW | DW | Mixed | Strict PW % | Broad PW % |
|--------|-----------|----|----|-------|-------------|------------|
| P1 (1/1/2022 -- 6/28/2024) | 456 | 82 | 319 | 55 | 18.0 | 30.0 |
| P2 (6/28/2024 -- 2/5/2025) | 116 | 9 | 92 | 15 | 7.8 | 20.7 |
| P3 (2/5/2025 -- present) | 317 | 34 | 257 | 26 | 10.7 | 18.9 |
| P2+P3 combined | 433 | 43 | 349 | 41 | 9.9 | 19.4 |
| All dated | 889 | 125 | 668 | 96 | 14.1 | 24.9 |

*Source: FHA Unified Database, disability cases (n=1,720).*

**Effect size:** The strict plaintiff win rate drops from 18.0% (P1) to 7.8% (P2) and 10.7% (P3). Combining the post-*Loper Bright* periods, the strict rate falls to 9.9% -- a 45% relative decline from the P1 baseline. The broad win rate (counting mixed outcomes as partial plaintiff victories) declines from 30.0% (P1) to 19.4% (P2+P3), a 35% relative collapse. The P2 transition period shows the sharpest drop on both measures, consistent with immediate doctrinal disruption followed by a modest P3 stabilization that nonetheless remains well below pre-*Loper Bright* levels.

### B.2 Binary Validation

As a robustness check, the three-period results are validated against a simple calendar-year binary split:

| Period | N | Strict PW % | Broad PW % |
|--------|---|-------------|------------|
| Pre (decision year <= 2023) | 709 | 21.0 | 34.3 |
| Post (decision year >= 2024) | 637 | 11.6 | 22.8 |

The binary split confirms the direction and approximate magnitude of the three-period findings: strict win rates fall by roughly 45% and broad win rates by roughly 34% after the doctrinal shift.

| P1 vs. P3 | 7.72 | 0.005 |

**Broad plaintiff win rate:**

| Comparison | chi-squared | p-value |
|-----------|------------|---------|

All pairwise comparisons reach conventional significance (p < 0.05). The combined P2+P3 comparison yields the strongest results on both measures, supporting the inference that the post-*Loper Bright* decline is not attributable to chance variation.

### B.6 Confounders and Alternative Explanations

The following confounders cannot be ruled out:

1. **Enforcement withdrawal.** HUD issued zero reasonable-cause charges in January--August 2025; the Fair Housing Initiatives Program (FHIP) was defunded; Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity (FHEO) staffing was reduced by approximately two-thirds. These enforcement shocks coincide temporally with P3 and may independently suppress plaintiff success by removing the federal enforcement backdrop that supports private litigation.
2. **Judicial composition.** Federal bench composition shifts may affect disability claim outcomes independently of deference doctrine.
3. **Selection effects.** If weaker claims are being filed or stronger ones settling, the observed decline could overstate the doctrinal shift. The 2026 partial rebound (28.1%, n=64) may reflect case-selection effects as marginal claims are deterred.
4. **COVID-era docket effects.** The 2020--2021 dip in win rates (20.0% and 13.4%, respectively) precedes *Loper Bright* by several years and may reflect pandemic-related disruptions to court operations or litigation patterns.
5. **Three-period boundary sensitivity.** The P2/P3 boundary (February 5, 2025) corresponds to the executive enforcement withdrawal and is analytically distinct from the *Loper Bright* cutoff. Disentangling the doctrinal effect (P2) from the enforcement-withdrawal effect (P3) requires further research.
