## Appendix B: Post-*Loper Bright* Analysis

### B.1 Pre/Post Comparison — Unified Dataset (Primary)

**Cutoff:** Calendar year 2024+ treated as post-*Loper Bright* (decision date: June 28, 2024).

Pre-2024 FHA merits win rate: 17.9%. Post-2024: 16.0%. 2025 specifically: 7.9%.

**Effect size:** The decline from 17.9% to 16.0% overall is modest, but the 2025 trough (7.9%) represents a pronounced collapse in plaintiff success, consistent with the *Loper Bright* deference-free environment taking full effect with a lag.

### B.2 Per-Theory Merits Win Rates

Per-theory merits win rates from the Unified Dataset are reported in Appendix E, Section E.1a (Table: Win Rates by Legal Theory).

### B.2.1 *Loper Bright* Focus: The 2025 Trough

The 2025 win rate of 7.9% (n=214 FHA merits claims) represents the lowest annual plaintiff success rate in the dataset. This trough is consistent with the *Loper Bright* deference-free environment taking full effect after a transition year (2024: 13.7%, n=153). The severity of the 2025 collapse — less than half the pre-2024 baseline of 17.9% — suggests that the removal of Chevron deference has had a pronounced effect on FHA disability enforcement outcomes.

### B.5 Confounders and Alternative Explanations

The following confounders cannot be ruled out:
1. **Enforcement withdrawal.** HUD issued zero reasonable-cause charges in January–August 2025; FHIP was defunded; FHEO staffing was reduced by approximately two-thirds.
2. **Judicial composition.** Federal bench composition shifts may affect disability claim outcomes independently of deference doctrine.
3. **Selection effects.** If weaker claims are being filed or stronger ones settling, the observed decline could overstate the doctrinal shift. The 2026 rebound (33.3%, n=54) may reflect case-selection effects as marginal claims are deterred.
4. **COVID-era docket effects.** The 2020 drop (16.7%) precedes *Loper Bright* by four years and may reflect pandemic-related disruptions to court operations or litigation patterns.
