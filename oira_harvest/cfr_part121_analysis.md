# 24 CFR Part 121 and Protected-Class Data-Collection Authority

**Memo resolving Gap 3.** 2026-04-18. Sources at `research_outputs/`.

## Headline

**Gap 3's factual premise is mistaken.** 24 CFR Part 121 is titled **"Collection of Data"** in the current eCFR and has been since 1989 — it was never retitled "Participation in HUD Programs by Faith-Based Organizations." The Faith-Based rule is at **24 CFR § 5.109**. HUD's 2022 "24 CFR 121" citation was accurate on its face and still is. The real question is whether § 121.2's text supports the "protected class data" proposition. It mostly does — § 121.2 enumerates race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, handicap, and family characteristics — but its authority block omits § 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, and it does not name SOGI on its face.

## Section 1 — 24 CFR Part 121 as of Sept. 27, 2022 (verbatim)

Source: `https://www.ecfr.gov/on/2022-09-27/title-24/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-121` (eCFR metadata: Title 24 > Subtitle B > Chapter I > Part 121). Full text saved at `research_outputs/24CFR121_as_of_2022-09-27.txt`.

> **PART 121 — COLLECTION OF DATA**
>
> **Authority:** Title VIII, Civil Rights Act of 1968 (42 U.S.C. 3600-3620); E.O. 11063, 27 FR 11527; sec. 602, Civil Rights Act of 1964 (42 U.S.C. 2000d-1); sec. 562, Housing and Community Development Act of 1987 (42 U.S.C. 3608a); sec. 2, National Housing Act, 12 U.S.C. 1703; sec. 7(d), Department of Housing and Urban Development Act, 42 U.S.C. 3535(d).
>
> **Source:** 54 FR 3317, Jan. 23, 1989, unless otherwise noted.
>
> **§ 121.1 Purpose.** The purpose of this part is to enable the Secretary of Housing and Urban Development to carry out his or her responsibilities under the Fair Housing Act, Executive Order 11063, dated November 20, 1962, title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, and section 562 of the Housing and Community Development Act of 1987. These authorities prohibit discrimination in housing and in programs receiving financial assistance from the Department of Housing and Urban Development, and they direct the Secretary to administer the Department's housing and urban development programs and activities in a manner affirmatively to further these policies and to collect certain data to assess the extent of compliance with these policies.
>
> **§ 121.2 Furnishing of data by program participants.** Participants in the programs administered by the Department shall furnish to the Department such data concerning the race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, handicap, and family characteristics of persons and households who are applicants for, participants in, or beneficiaries or potential beneficiaries of, those programs as the Secretary may determine to be necessary or appropriate to enable him or her to carry out his or her responsibilities under the authorities referred to in § 121.1.

Part 121 contains only these two sections — HUD's cross-cutting demographic-reporting rule.

## Section 2 — Historical trajectory

- **Promulgated** at 54 FR 3317 (Jan. 23, 1989), after the 1988 Fair Housing Amendments (adding handicap and familial status) and 1987 HCDA § 562.
- **Never renumbered, never retitled, never textually amended.** eCFR snapshots from 2017, 2022, and the current edition are identical. eCFR "Recent Changes" for Part 121 returns nothing. Substantively stable for ~37 years.
- **Source of the memo's confusion:** "Participation in HUD Programs by Faith-Based Organizations" is the caption of **24 CFR § 5.109** (implementing EOs 13279/13559/13831/14015), not Part 121. Gap 3 misreads the eCFR hierarchy.

## Section 3 — Was HUD's 2022 citation accurate?

**Accurate but incomplete.**

1. **Facially accurate** — Part 121 exists, is titled "Collection of Data," and § 121.2 enumerates race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, handicap, family characteristics.
2. **Statute-authority gap** — Part 121's authority block omits **§ 504 of the Rehabilitation Act (29 U.S.C. 794)**. Disability-specific data authority runs more cleanly through Part 8 (HUD's § 504 rule).
3. **Silent on SOGI** — § 121.2 does not name SOGI. The "protected class data" framing depends on reading "sex" in § 121.2 to include SOGI under *Bostock* and EO 13988 — a construction argument.

SAGE/Jones Day (`comment_SAGE.txt` lines 106-115) paraphrases § 121.2 accurately and makes exactly this *Bostock*/EO 13988 bridge; the citation to "Sections 121.1 and 121.2" tracks the regulatory text above.

## Section 4 — 24 CFR Part 8 (verbatim excerpts)

Source: `https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/part-8` (saved `research_outputs/24CFR8_current_raw.html`). **Section-numbering correction:** the methodology memo placed "self-evaluation" at § 8.55 and "transition plan" at § 8.56. Wrong. Self-evaluation = **§ 8.51**; transition plan = **§ 8.21(c)(4)** (parallels §§ 8.24, 8.25). **§ 8.55 is Part 8's data/recordkeeping anchor; § 8.56 is complaint/investigation procedure.**

**§ 8.51 Self-evaluation** (§ 504 implementing):
> (a) Each recipient shall, within one year of July 11, 1988, . . . (1) Evaluate its current policies and practices to determine whether, in whole or in part, they do not or may not meet the requirements of this part; (2) Modify any policies and practices that do not meet the requirements of this part; and (3) Take appropriate corrective steps to remedy the discrimination revealed by the self-evaluation.

**§ 8.55 Compliance information** (the Part 8 data-collection anchor):
> (b) *Compliance reports.* Each recipient shall keep such records and submit to the responsible civil rights official . . . timely, complete, and accurate compliance reports . . . as the responsible civil rights official . . . may determine to be necessary to enable him or her to ascertain whether the recipient has complied or is complying with this part. **In general, recipients should have available for the Department data showing the extent to which individuals with handicaps are beneficiaries of federally assisted programs.**
> (c) *Access to sources of information.* . . .

**§ 8.21(c)(4) Transition plan** (non-housing; parallels at 8.24(d), 8.25(c)):
> If structural changes to non-housing facilities will be undertaken to achieve program accessibility, a recipient shall develop, within six months of July 11, 1988, a transition plan . . . . The plan shall, at a minimum—(i) Identify physical obstacles . . .; (ii) Describe in details the methods . . .; (iii) Specify the schedule . . .; (iv) Indicate the official responsible . . .; and (v) Identify the persons or groups with whose assistance the plan was prepared.

**§ 8.56** is investigation procedure, not a data-generation rule.

## Section 5 — 24 CFR § 1.6 (verbatim, current)

Source: `https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-24/part-1/section-1.6` (saved at `research_outputs/24CFR1.6_current_raw.html`). Authority: 42 U.S.C. 2000d-1 and 3535(d); Source: 38 FR 17949, July 5, 1973.

> **§ 1.6 Compliance information.** (a) *Cooperation and assistance.* . . . (b) *Compliance reports.* Each recipient shall keep such records and submit to the responsible Department official or his designee timely, complete, and accurate compliance reports at such times, and in such form and containing such information, as the responsible Department official or his designee may determine to be necessary to enable him to ascertain whether the recipient has complied or is complying with this part 1. **In general, recipients should have available for the department racial and ethnic data showing the extent to which members of minority groups are beneficiaries of federally assisted programs.** (c) *Access to sources of information.* . . . (d) *Information to beneficiaries and participants.* . . .

**Scope.** Part 1 implements **Title VI** only — race, color, national origin. § 1.6(b) expressly names only "racial and ethnic data." Title VI does *not* reach disability, sex, religion, age, or SOGI. § 1.6 therefore does **not** extend to disability (that is Part 8/§ 504 territory), sex (Title IX/FHA), age (Part 146/ADA 1975), or SOGI. The 2023 Supporting Statement's § 1.6 citation is a narrow race/ethnicity authority only.

## Section 6 — Doctrinal recommendation (rank-ordered)

**1. § 121.2 — primary, transsubstantive anchor.** Only HUD rule enumerating race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, handicap, family characteristics in one data-collection command keyed to the Secretary's "necessary or appropriate" determination. Authority sweeps Title VIII, Title VI, HCDA § 562, E.O. 11063, HUD Act § 7(d).

**2. § 1.6(b) — secondary, Title VI race/ethnicity anchor.** The 2023 Supporting Statement already cites it; accept. Narrow scope (race, color, national origin) — do not over-read.

**3. § 8.55(b) — secondary, § 504 disability-data anchor.** Stronger disability hook than § 121.2's bare "handicap" because Part 8 implements § 504 (the statute missing from Part 121's authority block).

**4. § 5.109 — NOT a data-collection anchor.** Faith-based equal-participation rule. Do not conflate with Part 121.

**5. SOGI.** § 121.2 does not name SOGI. Bridge via *Bostock* + EO 13988 reading of "sex" — present as construction, not text.

**Bottom line.** HUD's 2022 "24 CFR 121" citation is correct, not renumbered, not sloppy. Strongest posture: § 121.2 as transsubstantive authority, supplemented by § 1.6(b) (race/ethnicity), § 8.55(b) (disability), with EO 13988 for SOGI. Gap 3 dissolves.
