## Revised Empirical Narrative

### What's Strong (build around these)

1. **Disability dominance at 66.2%** — This is the headline. Two-thirds of federal FHA litigation involves disability. The AFFH apparatus addresses none of it. This is not a niche argument.

2. **Structural claim-type difference** — 53.6% RA denial (disability) vs. 77.6% disparate treatment (race). These are different statutory regimes requiring different enforcement infrastructure. AFFH was built for § 3604(a); disability runs through § 3604(f)(3)(B).

3. **Post-*Loper Bright* decline: 34.6% → 25.3%** — Significant at p=0.018. The year-by-year shows stability at ~34% through 2023, then a sharp drop starting in 2024. Cleaner than the old data which had confounding 2020 COVID dip.

4. **Interactive process = success predictor** — When courts discuss the interactive process, plaintiff success nearly doubles (49.3% vs 28.2%). This directly supports your argument that § 3604(f)(3)(B) enforcement should focus on interactive process compliance.

5. **Housing authority failure** — The worst defendant type at 16.1% success. Public entities that should face heightened § 3608(d) obligations yield the worst plaintiff outcomes. This is your enforcement gap argument in one number.

6. **Geographic enforcement deserts** — NY (19.4%) and CA (21.9%) despite having the most cases. IL (58.6%) and FL (43.5%) doing much better. Supports the argument that enforcement outcomes depend on local infrastructure.

### What's Weak (reframe or drop)

1. **~~Iqbal disparity~~** — Race cases cite Iqbal more. Drop the 3.3x claim. You can still note that Iqbal is associated with a +4.7pp increase in defendant wins overall (p=0.035), but it's not a disability-specific problem.

2. **~~Disability loses more than race~~** — They don't. Both lose at ~76%. The story is that *everyone* loses, not that disability loses more.

3. **~~Substance use double-gatekeeping as worst category~~** — Substance use at 42.5% PW+MX is actually *above average*. MULTIPLE_UNSPECIFIED at 21.0% is the worst. The double-gatekeeping argument might still hold conceptually but the outcome data doesn't support it being the most disadvantaged category. Consider reframing: organizational plaintiffs (sober living homes) may be pulling the substance use average up while individual claimants still suffer — you'd need plaintiff_type cross-tabulation to confirm.

4. **Pre/post decline causation** — The 9.3pp decline is real and significant, but the year-by-year shows the drop starts in 2024 (before most *Loper Bright* effects could manifest in decided opinions). The 2026 data (35.7%, n=28) may suggest a rebound, though the sample is too small to confirm.

### New Finding Worth Adding

**MIXED outcomes as the disability difference:** Disability strict-PW is only 5.9% in the recentFHA DB, but PW+MIXED is 23.7%. This means disability plaintiffs are winning partial victories at very high rates — 17.8% of decided disability cases result in mixed outcomes (vs. 21.3% for race). This suggests disability claims are doctrinally viable but courts are splitting the baby rather than ruling outright for plaintiffs. This supports an argument about doctrinal uncertainty in accommodation analysis — courts aren't sure what "reasonable" means, so they grant partial relief.
