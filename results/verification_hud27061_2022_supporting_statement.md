## Bottom line

I did **not** recover a separate public `2022` supporting statement for the broader `Race and Other Demographic Data Reporting Form` concept. What I did recover is enough to tighten the record in three important ways:

1. Reginfo's public OMB history for control number `2535-0113` shows no separate public 2022 OIRA submission. The relevant visible submission is `ICR reference no. 202301-2535-001`, received `01/27/2023` and concluded `06/13/2023`.
2. The public supporting-documents page for that 2023 ICR contains a final supporting statement (`updated 5/11/2023`) and the 60-day and 30-day Federal Register notices. The final supporting statement cites `42 U.S.C. § 3608(e)(6)` and the Fair Housing Act as authority, but operationally describes a race-and-ethnicity collection keyed to SPD 15 / Title VI; it does **not** mention `24 C.F.R. Part 121`, disability fields, or broader protected-class collection.
3. The public comment emails preserved on reginfo show that an outside commenter specifically asked HUD for `the proposed instrument referenced at https://www.regulations.gov/document/HUD-2006-0214-0007 — the new Race and Other Demographic Data Reporting Form.` That is strong evidence that a broader proposed instrument existed in the 2022 60-day stage, even though I could not retrieve it directly because regulations.gov was blocked in this environment and its API requires a key.

Practical conclusion:
- the public record strongly supports the claim that HUD publicly floated a broader 2022 expansion and then narrowed the collection back to race/ethnicity in the 2023 OIRA submission;
- the public record does **not yet** give us the separate 2022 supporting statement or the broader 2022 instrument itself;
- a FOIA request remains the right next move if the note wants to sharpen the arbitrariness / unexplained-reversal point beyond the Federal Register notices.

Confidence:
- public proposal-and-withdrawal sequence: HIGH
- existence of a broader proposed instrument in the 2022 stage: MEDIUM-HIGH
- recovery of a distinct public 2022 supporting statement: NEGATIVE / not located

This is a useful tightening point: by the time HUD submitted the active ICR to OIRA, the public supporting statement had reverted to race/ethnicity framing even while still quoting § 3608(e)(6) as background authority.

## 2. Burden-hour finding

The public record recovered here does **not** show a burden-hour increase tied to the broader 2022 language.

### 30-day notice / final 2023 record
Recovered burden estimate:
- respondents: `14,375`
- frequency: `1.2`
- annual responses: `17,250`
- burden per response: `0.50 hours`
- annual burden hours: `8,625`

The hourly wage / cost assumptions changed between the 60-day and 30-day materials, but the burden-hours figure did not. So the narrow public claim I am comfortable making is:

`The public notices recovered in this run do not show any increase in annual burden hours between the 2022 broader-proposal notice and the 2023 narrowed submission.`

That is stronger than an inference-free observation, but weaker than saying HUD formally found `zero marginal burden` for specific disability fields in a distinct 2022 supporting statement. I do **not** have the latter document.
